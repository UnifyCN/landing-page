For many newcomers and international students, building a life in Canada starts with figuring out a new place. While your long-term goal may be a higher paying professional career, you can often use this time effectively — building Canadian work experience, skill, and qualifying yourself for Canadian immigration.

These steps help newcomers gain Canadian work experience, reach career targets below their experience level, and position themselves toward long-term career stability.

This post breaks Canadian permanent residency down into three categories:

1. Building Canadian Work Experience
2. Leveraging Immigration Pathways (Express Entry, PNP, AIP, and more)
3. Developing Long-Term Career Strategy

## What Counts as an Entry-Level Job for PR Purposes?

In Canada's immigration system, jobs are classified using the [National Occupational Classification (NOC)](https://www.canada.ca/en/immigration-refugees-citizenship/services/immigrate-canada/express-entry/eligibility/find-national-occupation-code.html) system, specifically the Training, Education, Experience and Responsibilities (TEER) classification — specifically TEER 2 and 3 in Canada's National Occupational Classification (NOC) system.

Most PR pathways require a TEER 2 or 3 occupation, or qualifications through an employment program.

| TEER | Examples | PR Eligibility via Express Entry? |
|------|----------|----------------------------------|
| TEER 0 | Senior Manager, Director | Yes |
| TEER 1 | Engineers, Accountants, Nurses | Yes |
| TEER 2 | Cooks, Electricians, Store Manager | Yes |
| TEER 3 | Administrative assistants; early childhood educators | Yes |
| TEER 4 | Food Counter Attendants, Delivery Driver | Not Referral; some |
| TEER 5 | General Labourers, Harvesting Worker | No Referral |

**Key stat:** As of 2025, [Express Entry category-based selection](https://www.canada.ca/en/immigration-refugees-citizenship/services/immigrate-canada/express-entry/submit-profile/rounds-invitations/category-based-selection.html) draws select for the primary workforce, meaning many recent international students and newcomers are now working through programs specifically for meeting TEER 2, 3 requirements, and typically TEER 4 eligible Canadian experience must still be met.

## Stage 1: How to Build Canadian Work Experience

Before PR is possible, most newcomers need a foundation. Canadian work experience is a critical step.

### 1. Target TEER 2 and TEER 3 Roles Strategically

Many entry-level positions are accessible without advanced Canadian credentials or work history. Common sectors:

- Food service supervisors: TEER 2
- Cooks: TEER 2
- Dental reception, assistant: TEER 3
- Childcare workers: TEER 3
- Construction trades apprentices: TEER 2
- Office admin assistants: TEER 3

### 2. Accumulate the Required Hours of Work Experience

Most PR pathways require at least **1,560 hours** (approximately 1 year) of full-time paid work within the past five years:

- No less than one continuous work period
- Part-time experience is also accepted when documented
- Be documented with employment records, pay stubs, and reference letters

### 3. Maintain Legal Status Throughout Employment

Ensuring that your immigration status was maintained while you legally worked in Canada:

- Keep your work permits renewed at least 90 days before expiry
- Always apply at least 30 days before the permit expires
- Have all permits and immigration documents accessible at all times at [cic.gc.ca](https://www.canada.ca/en/immigration-refugees-citizenship.html)
- Immediately contact IRCC if your status is at risk

### 4. Strengthen Language and Integration Skills

Being tested for citizenship (CLB level, CLB 4–8 for TEER 2, CLB 6–8 for TEER 3):

- Language tests taken within 2 years
- Canada's minimum core: CLB 4 (French or English) or CLB 7 at TEER 3
- PNP streams: by province some have lower, some specifically CLB 5 or above

## Stage 2: How to Leverage Immigration Pathways

There are several key immigration programs for newcomers who have entry-level job experience. A summary of the most important immigration programs:

### 1. Canadian Experience Class (CEC) — Express Entry

The [Canadian Experience Class (CEC)](https://www.canada.ca/en/immigration-refugees-citizenship/services/immigrate-canada/express-entry/eligibility/canadian-experience-class.html) is one of the most straightforward and reliable pathways for people already living and working in Canada if they have completed at least 1 year of skilled work experience.

**Who it's for:**

- No proof of employment funds required
- Processing: 6 months to 18 months in key
- Typical cost: approximately 9 months in key

Since April 2026, Canada's category-based [Express Entry](https://www.canada.ca/en/immigration-refugees-citizenship/services/immigrate-canada/express-entry.html) draws are now the dominant driver. Draws are now semi-automatically or category-based when conducted in Canada within the past year.

### 2. Provincial Nominee Programs (PNP)

[Provincial Nominee Programs (PNP)](https://www.canada.ca/en/immigration-refugees-citizenship/services/immigrate-canada/provincial-nominees.html) provide secondary streaming and also serve those who have specific connections to particular regions through Provincial Nominee Programs. These include:

- **British Columbia:** healthcare, tech, hospitality or social services
- **Manitoba:** Skilled and in-service streams for the Manitoba province
- **Alberta:** Supporting Canadian agriculture, skilled trades, and hospitality

These programs are aimed at immigrant workers with specific job offers or specific connections to a specific employer.

### 3. Atlantic Immigration Program (AIP)

The [Atlantic Immigration Program (AIP)](https://www.canada.ca/en/immigration-refugees-citizenship/services/immigrate-canada/atlantic-immigration.html) supports employment in New Brunswick, Nova Scotia, Prince Edward Island, or Newfoundland and Labrador.

Designed for: Both intermediate and entry-level skilled workers in roles that cannot be filled from within Canada.

### 4. Rural and Northern Immigration Pilot (RNIP)

Launched in 2019 as a successor to the Atlantic Immigration Pilot. RNIP plans to process 1,000 candidates per year under the community recommendation framework.

### 5. Caregiver Immigration Programs

Canada has dedicated immigration pathways for workers in caregiving roles. The [Home Care Worker Immigration Pilots](https://www.canada.ca/en/immigration-refugees-citizenship/services/immigrate-canada/caregivers.html) are designed to assist PR-eligible employees. These programs are designed to assist PR-eligible employees without requiring advanced TEER experience.

## Stage 3: Build Long-Term Career Strategy as a Newcomer

Entry-level jobs are the start of a long-term career path. Here's how to maximize them:

### 1. Build a Strong Employment Record

Your employment record matters — not just your job title. Employers who have great employment records become the most PR-eligible applicants, which means keeping:

- Employment income records (company information)
- Pay stubs, dates, hours, bonus, title, level and branch level
- Record of dates and all Notices of Employment (NOE) and T4 slips

Keep detailed employment records of your dates, hours, bonus, and tier. Reliable detailed employment records, especially your role, NOE, and T4, mean your immigration file is more easily reviewed.

### 2. Track Every Hour You Work

Your employment record must track dates and hours at the company level at all times:

- Pay stubs and company statements (required per application)
- T4 slips and Notice of Employment (NOE): required for AIP applications
- Reliable employment records: your company's statement is typically only one of several required documents

Each TEER 0–3 application will require documentation at some point, which is why every TEER application requires you to track your employment level.

### 3. Plan Your Career Progression Toward TEER 2 or 3

If your role at this time is TEER 4 or 5, you must start planning towards your TEER 3 pathway before applying to the CEC:

- Research the NOC requirements for your industry before applying
- Ask your employer how you can grow in your role — for example, asking for supervisory roles or assigning higher skills roles
- Apply for co-op programs whenever possible to get references that show your TEER level
