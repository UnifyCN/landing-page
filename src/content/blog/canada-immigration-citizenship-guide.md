## Understanding Canada's Immigration System

Canada's immigration system operates through four major streams: Economic, Family, Refugee, and Other. The government issued 1,365,000 permanent admissions target for 2027. Family Class openings for non-economic admissions continue to expand considerably through 2027. Canada sets eligibility requirements for each pathway. Levels Plan 2027 outlines goals for each stream.

## Student Visa & Study Permits

Since January 2025, study permit applications follow a Provincial International Student Permit procedure. Applicants must obtain a [Designated Learning Institution (DLI)](https://www.canada.ca/en/immigration-refugees-citizenship/services/study-canada/study-permit/prepare/designated-learning-institutions-list.html) acceptance before applying for a study permit.

**Eligibility and Requirements:**

Since January 2025, most study permit applicants must obtain at least a Provincial study permit before submitting their online study application. The DLI criteria has stabilized, representing a significant improvement in application success rates.

Financial requirements have increased significantly. Students must demonstrate access to at least CAD $20,000 beyond tuition in the first year. Biometrics are required for most applicants.

The study permit application process begins online at canada.ca with supporting documents from the Designated Learning Institution. You'll need to provide biometrics at a Visa Application Collection Centre.

## Work Rights & Post-Graduation

International students with a valid study permit can work off campus up to 20 hours per week during sessions and full-time during breaks. You don't apply separately for this work authorization — it's included in your study permit.

The [Post-Graduation Work Permit (PGWP)](https://www.canada.ca/en/immigration-refugees-citizenship/services/study-canada/work/after-graduation/about.html) represents one of the most valuable aspects of studying in Canada for international students. After completing a full-time program at a DLI, you can apply for a PGWP for up to three years. This provides valuable Canadian work experience that frequently makes you competitive for permanent residency through the [Canadian Experience Class (CEC)](https://www.canada.ca/en/immigration-refugees-citizenship/services/immigrate-canada/express-entry/eligibility/canadian-experience-class.html).

Financial requirements for students have increased significantly. As of 2025, single students must show access at least $20,000 CAD plus their first year of tuition. This is a significant increase from the previous $10,000 requirement.

## Economic Immigration Pathways

### Express Entry System

[Express Entry](https://www.canada.ca/en/immigration-refugees-citizenship/services/immigrate-canada/express-entry.html) manages three federal economic immigration programs. After registering, you receive a Comprehensive Ranking System (CRS) score and enter a pool. IRCC issues Invitations to Apply (ITAs) in rounds of draws from the pool.

**Since April 2024,** Canada has moved to category-based selection draws. The dominant pathway for many applicants is the French-language proficiency or specific occupations categories. Applicants with strong language scores (CLB 9+) and relevant work experience have the highest success rates.

Processing time: If completed in 6 months or less — often significantly faster.

### Provincial Nominee Programs (PNPs)

[Provincial Nominee Programs](https://www.canada.ca/en/immigration-refugees-citizenship/services/immigrate-canada/provincial-nominees.html) allow provinces and territories to nominate individuals for permanent residence who meet specific regional labour market needs. A provincial nomination adds 600 points to your CRS score, virtually guaranteeing an ITA from Express Entry.

Key PNP streams with strong uptake:

- **British Columbia:** Tech pilot, healthcare, and skilled trades streams
- **Ontario:** In-Demand Skills stream targeting agriculture, food production, and similar sectors
- **Manitoba:** Skilled Worker in Manitoba stream
- **Alberta:** Advantage streams targeting construction and hospitality workers

### Atlantic Immigration Program (AIP)

[The Atlantic Immigration Program (AIP)](https://www.canada.ca/en/immigration-refugees-citizenship/services/immigrate-canada/atlantic-immigration.html) supports employment in New Brunswick, Nova Scotia, Prince Edward Island, or Newfoundland and Labrador. Employers in Atlantic Canada must be designated to participate. The program has no points-based system — instead, it focuses on a job offer from a designated employer.

### Atlantic Immigration Pilot & Rural Pilots

The [Rural and Francophone Community Immigration Pilots](https://www.canada.ca/en/immigration-refugees-citizenship/services/immigrate-canada/rural-franco-pilots.html) help smaller communities attract and retain newcomers. Communities themselves recommend candidates based on local labour market needs. RNIP plans to process 1,000 candidates per year.

## Spouse and Partner Sponsorship

[Canadian sponsors](https://www.canada.ca/en/immigration-refugees-citizenship/services/application/application-forms-guides/guide-5289-sponsor-your-spouse-common-law-partner-conjugal-partner-dependent-child-complete-guide.html) — citizens and permanent residents — can sponsor their spouse, common-law partner, or conjugal partner for permanent residence.

**Eligibility:** Sponsors must be at least 18 years old and be a Canadian citizen or permanent resident. Sponsors cannot be in receipt of social assistance (except for disability).

**Spouse and Common-Law Partner Sponsorship:**

The process between initiating and completing a sponsorship application requires considerable documentation and attention. A marriage certificate alone is insufficient — officers assess the genuineness of the relationship and document that both parties intend to cohabit.

Processing time is significantly longer than processing times for many other categories — 12 months for inland applications as of February 2026. Processing times are significantly longer for applicants in certain high-demand countries; processing make target wait times significantly longer. Applications that are incomplete or inadmissible can make processing times significantly longer.

## Parents and Grandparents Program (PGP)

The [Parents and Grandparents Program](https://www.canada.ca/en/immigration-refugees-citizenship/services/immigrate-canada/family-sponsorship/sponsor-parents-grandparents/apply.html) is one of the most popular immigration categories, but one of the most competitive. More than 24,000 applications are submitted for what averages a fraction of available spots each intake. Parents and grandparents sponsored must meet an income threshold. Canadian citizens and permanent residents must demonstrate they meet the minimum necessary income for their household size — including the parents or grandparents being sponsored — at the time of application and for the three years prior.

1st families who don't qualify for the PGP under a quota system: The Super Visa that allows a 5-year stay may be available as an alternative. The Super Visa requires a private health insurance policy but allows parents to stay for up to 5 years.

## Dependent Children and Other Relatives

[Dependent children](https://www.canada.ca/en/immigration-refugees-citizenship/services/immigrate-canada/age-limit-requirements-dependent-children.html) under 22 years of age may be sponsored. Children must be single, not in a common-law relationship, and financially dependent on parents.

Children 22 years and older may qualify if they are financially dependent on their parents due to a physical or mental condition.

Other relatives — including siblings, nephews, nieces, aunts, uncles, or cousins — may not be sponsored directly unless there is no eligible sponsor available in a closer relationship. These provisions are narrow and rarely approved.
