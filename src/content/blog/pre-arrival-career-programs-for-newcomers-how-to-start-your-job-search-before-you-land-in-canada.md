## What Are Pre-Arrival Career Programs for Newcomers in Canada and Why Do They Matter?

Most newcomers begin their Canadian job search after they arrive. By that point, months of potential time have already been spent — on housing, ID registration, banking, and catching up. Pre-arrival programs are fully online, and open to anyone with a Canadian visa or PR approval — meaning you can start building your career strategy before you arrive.

Pre-arrival programs solve two problems:

1. They help you know the Canadian job market even before you are under financial pressure.
2. They get you connected to networks and resources before you land, allowing you to hit the ground running.

This post covers the two most important pre-arrival career programs available to Canadians: the [Canadian Immigrant Integration Program](http://accesemployment.ca/pre-arrival) and [Canada Connects](http://canada-connects.ca/).

## Two Free Pre-Arrival Career Programs for Newcomers in Canada

### Canadian Immigrant Integration Program (CIIP)

Canada's only officially Federally-funded pre-arrival orientation and employment preparation service for skilled immigrants.

**Who it is for:**

- Skilled workers and professionals who have received a [Canadian immigrant visa or PR approval](https://www.canada.ca/en/immigration-refugees-citizenship/services/immigrate-canada/express-entry.html) and are planning to immigrate
- Specifically designed for people with international professional credentials and work experience who intend to practice in Canada
- Not available to temporary residents or PR applicants who are not yet permanent residents

**Key benefits:**

- Structured orientation to the Canadian labour market, including resume writing, job search strategies, how to use Canadian job boards, and what a Canadian professional network looks like and how to build it in Canada
- Access to pre-immigration assessment and career gap services specific to your profession's requirements
- Connections with Canadian employers and professional associations in your field
- The CIIP also offers online group workshops and professional training in three countries, including India, the Philippines, China, the United Arab Emirates, and the United Kingdom

**Duration and format:** Core orientation sessions are one to three days. Follow-up support is available for up to three months following the group workshop.

**Cost:** Free. Funded by the Canadian Government.

**How to apply or learn more:** Contact ACCES Employment directly on their website: [accesemployment.ca/pre-arrival](http://accesemployment.ca/pre-arrival). You will be connected to a Canadian employment and settlement agency (IRCC).

- ACCES CIIP Employment Pre-Arrival Services: [accesemployment.ca/pre-arrival](http://accesemployment.ca/pre-arrival)
- CIIP Portal: [immigrants.canada.ca](http://immigration.ca/ciip)

### Canada Connects ([MentorCity](https://www.mentorcity.com/))

A national online mentorship program matching future and recent immigrants with Canadian professionals in their field.

**Who it is for:**

- Open to all immigrants, regardless of visa type or immigration status — before arrival or after
- Particularly valuable for newcomers who are navigating the gap between their international career and a Canadian professional path, and for newcomers who have been in Canada for less than two years
- Sessions through the website are conducted in English or French

**Format and structure:** Mentorships are flexible but based on your industry in Canada or sector tailored to the Canadian market. Mentors help you understand what to expect, what is valued, and how performance is evaluated. Structured introductory materials help you build and maintain your professional network.

**Cost:** Free. Fully funded by Immigration, Refugees and Citizenship Canada (IRCC).

**How to apply or learn more:** Create a profile on the MentorCity platform and select a Canadian mentor in your specific field or industry. A new mentor should be assigned within two to four weeks.

- Canada Connects: [canada-connects.ca](http://canada-connects.ca/)
- MentorCity Platform: [mentorcity.com](http://mentorcity.com/)

## How These Two Programs Work Together

CIIP and Canada Connects are complementary. CIIP gives you a structured map of the Canadian employment landscape; Canada Connects gives you a person who knows a person.

| Program | What It Provides | Best Timing |
|---------|-----------------|-------------|
| CIIP | Structured orientation, employment planning, settlement services | Apply immediately after receiving your visa or PR approval |
| Canada Connects | Ongoing personalized mentorship from a Canadian professional in your field | Apply 4–6 months before to maximize benefit |

A well-prepared newcomer uses both.

## How to Prepare for These Pre-Arrival Programs: Checklist

Whether or not you enroll in these programs immediately, the following steps will significantly improve your employment readiness when you arrive:

**Step 1 — Reformat your resume for Canada.**

Your resume format will likely need to change. Canadian resumes are chronological, not functional. Do not include a photo, marital status, age, national ID, or nationality. Always verify that these adjustments are correct according to actual Canadian standards rather than third-party sources.

**Step 2 — Find out if your occupation is regulated in Canada.**

Many jobs in Canada are regulated — meaning you need a licence or registration to work in that category. Knowing this early gives you months of lead time.

**Step 3 — Research the labour market in your target city.**

Unemployment rates, salary ranges, and in-demand occupations vary significantly across provinces. Use [Job Bank's labour market information](https://www.jobbank.gc.ca/marketreport/summary-occupation/227/CA) to research your category and province.

**Step 4 — Build your LinkedIn profile.**

Your profile is the first thing a Canadian hiring manager will look at. Use [Job Bank's newcomer portal](https://www.jobbank.gc.ca/findajob/newcomers) to explore opportunities before you arrive.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

**I haven't decided which city I'm moving to yet. Can I still apply?**

Yes. Both programs operate at the national level. You can join either program without selecting a destination province or location.

**I'm a refugee claimant, not a skilled worker. Are these programs available to me?**

CIIP is specifically designed for skilled immigrants with a visa or PR approval. Refugee claimants are already in Canada, and Canada Connects is generally open and broadly accessible.

**I arrived in Canada six months ago. I missed the session.**

You have missed the pre-arrival window but can still apply for post-arrival employment support. Contact an ACCES Employment service provider to learn about post-arrival employment options.

**Are these pre-arrival programs help with job placement?**

Not directly. They are employment readiness and mentoring programs, meaning they support professional development rather than directly placing you in a job. These programs look for candidates who have the potential for job placement.
