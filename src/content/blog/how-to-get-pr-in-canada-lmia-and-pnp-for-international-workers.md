*Last updated: June 2025 | Reading time: 8 min | Written for: International workers and newcomers in Canada on a work permit or PGWP*

When employers choose to hire internationally, their job postings are edited to occupations that are significantly higher paying, demonstrating specifically that the position cannot be filled locally.

Moving from a non-skilled to a skilled immigration pathway as an international worker in Canada is one of the most powerful strategic moves an international worker can make. It increases your earning potential and unlocks more direct pathways to Canadian PR. This guide breaks down exactly how to use them.

## What Is the NOC System and Why Does It Matter for PR?

The [National Occupational Classification (NOC) 2021 system](https://noc.esdc.gc.ca/) is Canada's official system for categorizing all occupations. Each job is classified according to a Training, Education, Experience and Responsibilities (TEER) category. An understanding of Canada's immigration tools — the Labour Market Impact Assessment (LMIA) and the Provincial Nominee Programs (PNPs) — is essential. This guide breaks down exactly how to use them.

| TEER Level | Examples | PR Eligible via Express Entry? |
|-----------|---------|-------------------------------|
| TEER 0 | Senior Manager, Director | Yes |
| TEER 1 | Engineers, Accountant, Nurse | Yes |
| TEER 2 | Cooks, Electricians, Store Manager | Yes |
| TEER 3 | Administrative Assistant, Shift Manager | Yes |
| TEER 4 | Food Counter Attendant, Delivery Driver | No: Referral |
| TEER 5 | General Labourers, Harvesting Worker | No |

Any transition to competitive Federal ([Express Entry](https://www.canada.ca/en/immigration-refugees-citizenship/services/immigrate-canada/express-entry.html)) PR — requires at least one of these: TEER 0, 1, 2, or 3.

**Key insight for newcomers:** You don't need to find a new employer to change your TEER classification — if you're also approved in a TEER 0, 1, 2, or 3 position within your current workplace — it still counts.

## 1. Climbing the TEER Ladder: The Skilled Work Experience Strategy

To qualify for [Express Entry](https://www.canada.ca/en/immigration-refugees-citizenship/services/immigrate-canada/express-entry.html) draws and improve your Comprehensive Ranking System (CRS) score, you need to work in a TEER 0, 1, 2, or 3 role — even if you currently hold a TEER 4 or 5 role.

**The TEER Ladder Strategy:**

- **Current Role:** TEER 4 or 5 (e.g., Food Counter Attendant, Delivery Driver, General Server)
- **Target Role:** TEER 3 (e.g., Food Service Supervisor, Store Manager, Front Desk Supervisor)
- **Timeline to PR eligibility:** 12 months in designated occupations + [CEC eligible](https://www.canada.ca/en/immigration-refugees-citizenship/services/immigrate-canada/express-entry/eligibility/canadian-experience-class.html) = Check your occupation

## 2. The "Promotion" LMIA Strategy (Step-by-Step)

A "Promotion LMIA" is an informal term for a strategy where your employer applies for a Labour Market Impact Assessment (LMIA) specifically for an internal promotion to fill a new closed work permit tied to the new class role. It is a **"dual intent"** permit — it can both retain you as an employee while also helping you qualify for permanent residency.

**How a Promotion LMIA works:**

**Step 1 — Prove Your Value:** After gaining experience and trust in your current TEER 4 or 5 role, approach your employer about a TEER 2 position. Frame it as solving their staffing problem — good supervisors are difficult to find, so it's important that this position cannot be filled by a CDN worker.

**Step 2 — Apply for the LMIA:** Your employer will submit the [High-Wage LMIA application](https://www.canada.ca/en/employment-social-development/services/foreign-workers/median-wage/high/applying.html) online. A new closed work permit is issued to you.

**Step 3 — Begin Building Skilled Work Experience:** After 12 months in your new TEER 2 or 3 role, you become CEC eligible and can apply under Express Entry.

**Step 4 — Apply for PR:** At this point, submitting a strong Express Entry profile with a [CRS score](https://www.canada.ca/en/immigration-refugees-citizenship/services/immigrate-canada/express-entry/check-score.html) becomes your primary focus.

## 3. The Promotion Conversation Checklist

Preparing to ask your employer for a promotion and an LMIA-backed upgrade is stressful, especially as a newcomer. Use this checklist:

- **Strategy goal:** Have you shown your employer you are meeting your existing performance targets?
- **Value clarity:** Do you understand that the $1,000 LMIA fee is typically the employer's responsibility?
- **Provide a reason:** Outline key impacts for the team and with team performance
- **Work permit:** You are responsible for your own work permit and visa application preparation and timing

## 4. Leveraging Provincial Nominee Programs (PNPs) as Stepping Stones

If an LMIA promotion isn't available through your employer, Provincial Nominee Programs offer an alternative. These programs are specifically designed for TEER 4 and 5 workers.

| Province | Stream | TEER Min. | Key Sections |
|----------|--------|-----------|---------|
| British Columbia | [Entry Level & Semi-Skilled](https://www.welcomebc.ca/Immigrate-to-B-C/BC-PNP-Skills-Immigration/Entry-Level-and-Semi-Skilled) | TEER 4 & 5 | Job offer + Construction, Tourism, Hospitality |
| Manitoba | In-Demand | TEER 4 & 5 | Job offer + Construction |
| Ontario | [In-Demand Skills Streams (OINP)](https://www.ontario.ca/page/oinp-employer-job-offer-in-demand-skills-stream) | TEER 4 & 5 | Sector-specific occupations, agriculture and food production |
| Alberta | [Advantage + Tourism and Hospitality](https://www.alberta.ca/alberta-advantage-immigration-program-tourism-hospitality-stream) | TEER 4 & 5 | Job offer + Construction, Tourism, Hospitality |
| Nova Scotia | [Occupations in Demand](https://novascotiaimmigration.com/move-here/nova-scotia-nominee-program/) | TEER 3 | Permanent offer only — Healthcare support, Transport, Cleaning |

**What each stream offers:**

**British Columbia — Entry Level & Semi-Skilled:** If you work in tourism, hospitality, or food processing, you may be eligible for BC PNP for at least one year. This helps you understand your gaps and whether a Provincial Nominee Programs application is possible for at least six months. Employer needs a job offer for you for six months. Then you're eligible for a PNP nomination — without having to upgrade your TEER level.

**Ontario — In Demand Skills Streams (OINP):** The program targets [specific occupations](https://www.ontario.ca/page/oinp-employer-job-offer-in-demand-skills-stream) in agriculture, food processing, and caregiving. An OINP can get you a nomination that makes you eligible for Canadian permanent residency in 6–12 months (even higher in Express Entry for categories).

**Alberta — Tourism and Hospitality Stream:** For workers who have been with an Alberta employer in tourism or hospitality. They must have been working for at least 3 months above the Alberta minimum salary for each category.

**Nova Scotia — Occupations in Demand:** These occupations are mainly linked to healthcare support, transport, cleaning, and service roles that are high in demand. For applicants who meet the primary requirement, mainly for a TEER 3 role.

*Disclaimer: Province-specific program availability often changes. Always verify the latest program details on official provincial immigration portals.*

## Conclusion: Your Roadmap from Survival Job to Skilled Work to Canadian PR

Moving from a TEER 4 or 5 position to a skilled career in Canada is one of the most significant steps you will take as an international worker. Your next steps:

1. Identify your current [NOC code](https://noc.esdc.gc.ca/) at [noc.esdc.gc.ca](https://noc.esdc.gc.ca/)
2. Map the TEER 2 or 3 role in your current employer and Canadian experience
3. Check for PNP streams in your province for your target occupations
4. Review PNP streams in your province for TEER-friendly pathways
